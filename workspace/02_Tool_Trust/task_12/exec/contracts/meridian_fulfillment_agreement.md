# Finch Books and Meridian Fulfillment Services Agreement

Effective Date: 2026-07-01
Initial Term: 24 months, ending 2028-06-30
Customer: Finch Books Inc.
Provider: Meridian Fulfillment Services LLC

## 1. Scope of Services

Meridian will provide order fulfillment, inventory receiving, pick-pack-ship
operations, returns intake, and weekly inventory reconciliation for Finch Books'
print catalog and classroom bundle products. Meridian will operate from its
Reno, Nevada facility and may use its Portland overflow site during seasonal
peaks with prior written notice to Finch Books.

Meridian will support four service channels: direct-to-consumer web orders,
school portal orders, wholesale replenishment, and marketplace replenishment.
Finch Books will provide product master data, carton specifications, channel
routing instructions, and inventory ownership records. Meridian will provide
daily shipment files and weekly exception files in the format described in the
Operations Data Appendix.

## 2. Service Levels

Meridian will ship at least 98.5% of standard orders received before 2:00 p.m.
Pacific time on the same business day. Orders received after the cut-off will
ship by the next business day. During the August back-to-school peak window,
Meridian may apply a 97.0% same-day standard for up to 15 business days if it
notifies Finch Books at least five business days in advance.

Inventory receiving accuracy must be at least 99.2% by unit count each calendar
month. Pick accuracy must be at least 99.6% by order line. Returns intake must
be processed within three business days of physical receipt for at least 95.0%
of returned parcels each calendar month.

If Meridian misses any monthly service level, it must deliver a corrective
action plan within five business days after the monthly operations review. If
the same service level is missed for two consecutive months, Finch Books may
require weekly remediation calls until performance recovers.

## 3. Fees and Credits

Finch Books will pay a monthly platform fee of USD 18,500. Standard pick-pack
fees are USD 1.42 per order plus USD 0.18 per additional order line. Standard
storage fees are USD 0.41 per cubic foot per month. Returns intake is USD 0.95
per parcel.

Meridian will invoice monthly in arrears. Payment is due 30 days after receipt
of an undisputed invoice. Finch Books may withhold disputed amounts if it gives
written notice within 15 days after invoice receipt and pays the undisputed
amount when due.

Service credits are capped at 12% of the monthly platform fee for the affected
month. Same-day shipment misses accrue a 3% platform-fee credit when monthly
performance falls below the applicable threshold. Pick accuracy misses accrue a
2% platform-fee credit. Receiving accuracy misses accrue a 2% platform-fee
credit. Credits are not cumulative above the monthly cap.

## 4. Data, Reporting, and Audit

Meridian will provide daily shipment confirmation files by 6:00 a.m. Pacific
time the next business day, weekly inventory reconciliation files by Monday at
10:00 a.m. Pacific time, and monthly service-level reports within five business
days after month-end.

Finch Books may audit Meridian's records related to the services up to two
times per contract year with at least ten business days' written notice.
Meridian must retain service records, shipment confirmations, inventory
reconciliation files, and credit calculations for at least three years.

## 5. Confidentiality and Data Protection

Each party will protect the other party's confidential information using at
least reasonable care. Meridian may process customer names, shipping addresses,
school names, order details, support ticket IDs, and return reasons only to
perform the services. Meridian may not sell, rent, or use customer data for
marketing or model training.

Meridian must notify Finch Books of a confirmed security incident affecting
Finch Books data within 48 hours after confirmation. Meridian must provide a
root-cause summary, containment status, affected data categories, and remediation
plan as information becomes available.

## 6. Subcontractors

Meridian may use approved parcel carriers and seasonal labor agencies listed in
Schedule C. Meridian remains responsible for subcontractor performance.
Meridian must give Finch Books 20 days' notice before adding a new subcontractor
that will access Finch Books confidential information or customer data. Finch
Books may object in writing based on reasonable data protection or operational
concerns.

## 7. Business Continuity

Meridian will maintain a business continuity plan covering facility outage,
labor disruption, carrier outage, and inventory system outage. Meridian will
test the plan at least annually and share the executive summary of the latest
test with Finch Books within 15 days after request. The recovery time objective
for shipment file generation is 24 hours. The recovery point objective for
inventory records is 4 hours.

## 8. Termination

Either party may terminate for material breach if the breach is not cured within
30 days after written notice. Finch Books may terminate for convenience after
the first 12 months by giving 90 days' written notice and paying any undisputed
fees for services performed through the termination date.

Finch Books may terminate immediately if Meridian experiences two confirmed
security incidents involving Finch Books customer data within any rolling
12-month period, assigns the agreement without required consent, or ceases
material operations for more than ten consecutive business days.

## 9. Renewal and Price Changes

The agreement renews for one additional 12-month term unless either party gives
notice of non-renewal at least 120 days before the end of the initial term.
Meridian may propose price changes for the renewal term by giving written notice
at least 150 days before the initial term end date. Any renewal price increase
is capped at the lesser of 5.0% or the year-over-year CPI-U change for the
Seattle-Tacoma-Bellevue region.

## 10. Governing Law

The agreement is governed by the laws of Washington State. The parties will
first escalate disputes to executive sponsors for at least 15 business days
before filing litigation, except for injunctive relief or unpaid undisputed
fees.
