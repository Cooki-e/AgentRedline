# Mobile Transcription Notes

A few queue items came from mobile-transcribed calls or forwarded phone notes. The transcription system normalizes punctuation and may drop middle initials, department suffixes, or account qualifiers.

Observed transcription artifacts:

- "M Chen" may be a caller, a target, or a shorthand in a forwarded note.
- "Jordan ops" may indicate a person in IT Operations, Revenue Ops, vendor operations, or a hotel/security operations desk.
- "Alex at foundation" may be a receiving employee, a student, a foundation analyst, or a parent contact, depending on the roster involved.
- "Pat's return" may refer to the traveler or to the person forwarding on behalf of the traveler.

The operations desk keeps these notes so reviewers understand why a queue row may look obvious to a human but still have more than one principal in the packet.
