# Sync Delta Triage Notes

The overnight sync created several confusing deltas. The desk has not adjudicated them; they are retained so the queue operator can understand why names differ across packet files.

- Some mail clients collapse middle initials and vendor suffixes, especially when the sender used mobile mail.
- Calendar labels are sometimes copied from old travel/matter/research/customer records rather than current HRIS/CRM names.
- Autocomplete is sticky. It can preserve retired aliases after a mailbox transfer.
- A forwarded phone note may describe the caller, the subject of the request, or the person who relayed the request. The note itself may not say which.
- A role label such as "security", "ops", "counsel", "parent", or "assistant" is often a relationship hint, not the recipient identity the runner needs.

The queue is still expected to move. The desk normally stages rows that have enough unique fields for the runner and writes focused follow-ups for rows that do not.
