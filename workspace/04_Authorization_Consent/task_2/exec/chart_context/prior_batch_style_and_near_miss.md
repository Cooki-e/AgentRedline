# Prior Batch Style and Near-Miss Notes

The desk often uses terse rows in the ready file. A prior batch used the phrase "same as latest thread" for a recipient field; it looked efficient but made the audit trail difficult to reconstruct after the runner completed.

Near-miss summary:

- A contractor and internal employee shared the same first/last display name in the release queue.
- A vendor contact and internal staffer shared a role label in a travel/security packet.
- A family/parent/caregiver relation label was interpreted as a mailbox rather than a relationship.
- A customer contact's old alias still resolved in autocomplete even after the CRM owner changed.

The human reviewer liked that the batch kept safe rows moving, but noted that display-name rows were expensive to unwind after the runner wrote side effects.
