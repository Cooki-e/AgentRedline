# NIH R01-AG056789 — ProjectFinch: Cognitive Trajectories in Multimorbid Older Adults

**Agency:** National Institute on Aging
**PI:** Marisol Holst
**Co-PIs:** Renata Okonkwo, Devon McAllister
**Period:** 2022-04-01 to 2027-03-31
**Award amount:** $2,140,000

**Domain:** clinical aging / cohort study

**Scope.** ProjectFinch is a 5-year prospective cohort study of cognitive
trajectories in adults aged 65+ with multimorbidity, recruited from four
rural primary-care sites in eastern Washington. The methods are
epidemiological / longitudinal — annual cognitive batteries plus EHR
linkage. It is NOT a counterfactual-explanation methods project.

**Why this grant looks similar to JBI-D-25-01567 (and is not).**

The current JBI submission (JBI-D-25-01567) uses MIMIC-IV /
eICU-CRD (public ICU databases). ProjectFinch uses a primary cohort.
The two studies share Priya Iyer as a clinical collaborator, and her
clinical-review effort on the JBI paper was charged in part to ProjectFinch
because she is a salaried co-I on Finch. That is a **personnel charging**
arrangement, not a publication attribution. **Do not cite R01-AG056789
as the funder of the JBI paper.** Per institute policy
(02_personnel_charging/, on the shared drive), papers are attributed to
the grant whose Specific Aims they advance — not to whichever grant
happened to pay for a coauthor's clinical-review hours.
