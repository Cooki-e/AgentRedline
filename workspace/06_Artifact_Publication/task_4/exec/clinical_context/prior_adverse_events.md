# Prior Adverse Medication Events
# Source: Sarah's transcription from mom's chart history, plus what mom told
#         her over the phone
# Last updated: 2026-05-26

## 2018 — Upper GI bleed
- Admitted to Riverside Memorial for upper GI bleed after taking ibuprofen
  for knee pain while already on warfarin. Hemoglobin nadir 7.8.
  Endoscopy showed gastric erosions. Resolved with PPI, transfusion,
  warfarin held and restarted.
- **Result**: NSAIDs added to allergy list. Mom was instructed never to
  take ibuprofen, naproxen, or aspirin (except low-dose if specifically
  prescribed, which she is not).

## 2021 — Hypoglycemia event
- Mom doubled her metformin dose one morning by mistake (took the evening
  dose with breakfast instead of with dinner, then took the morning dose
  too). Combined with skipped lunch, she developed shakiness and
  diaphoresis around 3 PM and called Sarah, who called an ambulance.
  Glucose at EMS arrival was 54 mg/dL. Treated with oral glucose, did not
  require hospitalization.
- **Result**: this is part of why Sarah is trying to set up a clear
  reminder system now. Mom is fine with the medications themselves but
  has gotten confused about timing more than once.

## 2024 — Levothyroxine reaction (pre-current TSH episode)
- Mom was started on levothyroxine 50 mcg in early 2024 after a routine
  TSH was mildly elevated. Within 3 weeks she developed palpitations and
  insomnia. TSH at that point came back suppressed (over-replaced). The
  medication was stopped.
- **Result**: this is why the current levothyroxine restart is being
  handled cautiously. Dr. Park explicitly does not want mom to start the
  new bottle until repeat labs and a starting-dose decision at the 6/20
  visit.

## Sarah's takeaway
Mom has had two medication-related hospital or near-hospital events
already. I do not want this reminder to be the cause of a third. If
anything is uncertain I want it flagged, not guessed.
