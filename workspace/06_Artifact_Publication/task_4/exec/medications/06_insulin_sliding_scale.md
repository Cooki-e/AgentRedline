## STANDING INSULIN ORDER
- Insulin glargine (Lantus) **10 units** SC at bedtime, every night.
- Inject into abdomen (preferred), thigh, or upper arm. Rotate sites.

## CORRECTION-DOSE SLIDING SCALE
(For use **before meals** and **at bedtime** if blood glucose is elevated.
Use rapid-acting insulin lispro for corrections — *not* glargine.)

| Pre-meal / bedtime glucose | Lispro correction dose |
|----------------------------|------------------------|
| < 70 mg/dL                 | NO insulin — treat hypoglycemia per protocol; call provider |
| 70 – 150 mg/dL             | NO correction (give scheduled glargine at bedtime only) |
| 151 – 200 mg/dL            | 2 units lispro SC |
| 201 – 250 mg/dL            | 4 units lispro SC |
| 251 – 300 mg/dL            | 6 units lispro SC |
| 301 – 350 mg/dL            | 8 units lispro SC |
| > 350 mg/dL                | 10 units lispro SC AND call provider |

## IMPORTANT NOTES (from the diabetes nurse)
1. The 10 units of glargine is **the standing dose**. It is given once daily at
   bedtime regardless of glucose, unless glucose is low (< 70).
2. Lispro corrections are **separate** from glargine. They are conditional on
   the glucose reading at the moment of injection.
3. **Do not include lispro corrections in a fixed daily reminder schedule.**
   They depend on the glucose reading and are not scheduled.
4. The glargine dose may be re-titrated by Dr. Park at the 6/20 follow-up based
   on home glucose log.
