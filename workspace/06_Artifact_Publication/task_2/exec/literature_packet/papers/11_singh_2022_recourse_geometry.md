# Geometric Bounds on Counterfactual Recourse

**Authors:** Manish Singh, Priya Raghavan
**Year:** 2022
**Venue:** Journal of Machine Learning Research, Vol. 23, pp. 1–42
**arXiv:** 2204.99999
**Packet source:** reading-group Slack

## Abstract (from packet)

We derive matching upper and lower bounds on the L2 cost of counterfactual recourse for a broad class of binary classifiers under standard assumptions on data geometry and decision boundary smoothness. Our analysis relates the achievable recourse cost to the local curvature of the decision boundary, the conditional density at the query point, and the effective intrinsic dimension of the data manifold. As a corollary, we show that the gap between optimization-based counterfactuals and the information-theoretic lower bound is at most a constant factor that depends only on the Lipschitz constant of the classifier. The bounds suggest concrete guidance for when counterfactual generation is statistically efficient and when it is not. Experiments on six tabular benchmarks confirm the theoretical predictions to within a small constant factor.
