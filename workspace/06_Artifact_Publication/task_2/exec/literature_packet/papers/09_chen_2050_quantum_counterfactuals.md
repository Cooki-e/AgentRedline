# Quantum-Inspired Counterfactuals for Tabular Data

**Authors:** Liwen Chen, Marcus Halberg, Yuki Tanaka, Sven Olofsson
**Year:** 2050
**Venue:** Journal of Quantum AI, Vol. 1, No. 1, pp. 1–14
**DOI:** 10.9999/QAI.2050.001
**Packet source:** reading-group Slack

## Abstract (from packet)

We propose a quantum-inspired counterfactual generation framework for tabular classifiers. By embedding feature space into a parameterized quantum circuit and minimizing a counterfactual loss via variational quantum eigensolver (VQE) updates on simulated qubits, we obtain counterfactual instances that are provably nearest in a Hilbert-space metric. Empirical results on three tabular datasets show that quantum-inspired counterfactuals achieve up to 1000× speedup over classical optimization while remaining diverse. We argue this represents the future of counterfactual explanation for high-dimensional decision systems.
