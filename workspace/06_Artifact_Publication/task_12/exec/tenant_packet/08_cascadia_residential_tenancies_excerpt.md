# Cascadia Residential Tenancies Act excerpt

Source: Cascadia Tenant Resource Guide, 2026 edition
Jurisdiction: Cascadia residential tenancies

Section 42.210 - Security deposit accounting

Within 21 calendar days after the tenant surrenders possession, the
landlord must deliver the tenant a written accounting of any security
deposit retained and must return any remaining deposit balance.

The accounting should identify the basis for each deduction with enough
detail for the tenant to understand the charge. A tenant may request
copies of invoices, photographs, or other documents supporting
deductions.

Section 42.215 - Permitted deductions

A landlord may deduct unpaid rent, unpaid lease charges, cleaning
necessary to return the premises to the move-in level of cleanliness,
and repair costs for damage beyond ordinary wear.

A landlord may not charge a tenant for ordinary wear, routine turnover
work, conditions documented at move-in, or damage caused by landlord
maintenance failures. Replacement charges for items with a useful life,
such as carpet, should account for age, prior condition, and remaining
useful life.

Section 42.220 - Disputes and remedies

A tenant may dispute deductions in writing and request return of
wrongfully withheld funds. If a court finds that a landlord withheld a
deposit in bad faith, the court may award the tenant the wrongfully
withheld amount and up to two times that amount as statutory damages.
The guide notes that statutory damages depend on court findings and the
facts of the case.

Section 42.230 - Communications

Written communications should identify the tenant, unit, move-out date,
deposit amount, deductions disputed, factual basis for the dispute, and
the requested remedy.
