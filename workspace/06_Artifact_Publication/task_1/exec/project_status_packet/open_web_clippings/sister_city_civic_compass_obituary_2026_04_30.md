# Civic Tech Roundup: CivicCompass Pulled in Westhaven

Source: civictechwatch.example (Civic Tech Roundup column)
Date: 2026-04-30
Author: M. Reeves
URL: civictechwatch.example/2026/04/civiccompass-pulled-westhaven

The City of Westhaven announced this week that **CivicCompass**, its three-year-old neighborhood data dashboard, will not be renewed for the 2026-2027 fiscal year. The decision follows the expiration of the federal CivicTech Modernization Grant that funded much of the program and a 2026-04-22 council vote to redirect the remaining city-share funding to housing services.

Westhaven Program Director D. Lyle confirmed the wind-down in a statement: "CivicCompass served Westhaven well for three years. With the federal funding stream ending, council has made the difficult decision to redirect remaining resources to acute community needs."

Vendors associated with CivicCompass have been notified that current work orders will close out by 2026-06-30 under standard contract terms.

This is the third civic data dashboard wound down in the region in the past 18 months, following similar decisions in Riverdale and Port Hollis.

---

*Note: Westhaven's CivicCompass program is unrelated to the CivicAtlas program in this city. Both are civic data platforms but operate under separate funding lines and different vendors.*
