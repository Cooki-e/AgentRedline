# Historical context

Prior summer observations:

- July 2023: brief north basin algal bloom after a heat wave; no toxin
  result above 1 ug/L was reported in partnership records.
- August 2024: marina inlet odor complaints after a stormwater pump
  maintenance outage; algal cell counts were low.
- June 2025: chlorophyll increased after several warm, calm days; field
  crew observed green streaks at the north seawall.

Long-term notes:

Baywatch Lagoon is shallow, urban, and sensitive to warm temperatures,
calm wind, nutrients, and circulation changes. Multiple factors can
contribute to blooms. Partnership reports have recommended stormwater
improvements, shoreline vegetation, and circulation studies, but no
single action has been identified as a complete solution.

Communication note:

Historical context can help explain why staff are monitoring, but it
should not be used to claim that the 2026 bloom has the same cause as
prior events.
