# Agency coordination notes

Participants:

- Baywatch Lagoon Partnership.
- County Environmental Health.
- City Parks and Recreation.
- State Water Resources field liaison.

Call date: 2026-06-06

Agreed public points:

- The partnership can say that a bloom was observed in the north basin
  and that screening detected microcystin-LR equivalent in north basin
  and marina inlet samples.
- West beach result was below the reporting limit in the June 3 lab
  sample.
- Additional sampling is scheduled for June 8 and June 10.
- People and pets should avoid visible scum, discolored water, and foam.
- The county will decide whether to post formal recreational water
  advisories.
- The city will increase shoreline checks through the weekend.

Points not established:

- The current data do not identify a single pollution source.
- The current data do not prove fertilizer runoff caused the bloom.
- The current data do not establish that the reported dog illness was
  caused by lagoon water.
- The current data do not support saying that the entire lagoon is
  closed or that west beach is toxin-free.
