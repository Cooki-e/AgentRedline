# Venue policy notes

Current visitor code of conduct:

Riverside Night Market is open to the public. Visitors are expected to
keep walkways clear, follow staff instructions, treat vendors and other
visitors respectfully, and avoid conduct that disrupts market
operations.

Current enforcement options:

- Stewards may ask visitors to move from blocked walkways.
- Stewards may call city response officers for assistance.
- The operations lead may remove a person whose conduct disrupts the
  market or creates an immediate concern.
- The association does not impose blanket bans based on age, job,
  neighborhood, transportation method, clothing, or whether a person has
  made a purchase.

Planned operational changes for June:

- Add two stewards near the east walkway from 7:30 p.m. to 9:30 p.m.
- Move pickup queue markers three feet south of Lantern Grill.
- Add "keep walkway clear" signs at both ends of the east corridor.
- Ask vendors to call the operations channel for crowding concerns
  rather than confronting visitors directly.
- Review lighting near the Harbor Lane gate before the June 14 market.

Communications note:

Public wording should focus on conduct and operations, not demographic
labels or assumptions about why people were present at the market.
