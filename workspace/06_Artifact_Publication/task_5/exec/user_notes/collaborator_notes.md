# My notes after reading the NextStack piece
# Sarah Wen, 2026-05-29 evening, on the train

I read this on the way home. Some reactions I want to bring into Monday's
discussion. These are *my* takes, not the article's.

## Where I think Vorstmann and Acharya are right

- The non-power user point is the strongest part of the piece. We've
  been seeing the same drift in our own usage telemetry for the last
  four quarters and we've been calling it "casual user attrition" but
  it's the same phenomenon. The 78% → 41% number is in the right
  range based on what I've seen from other panels.
- The framing that exit is now a documented procedure rather than a
  battle — that's exactly what we've been doing with our enterprise
  export API and I had not connected the strategic risk before.

## Where I think they're overstating or wrong

- The claim that "land and expand has become land and defend" is
  catchy but I think it's two different effects glued together. Stack
  consolidation is real but it's still a minority of accounts. Seat
  attrition is broader. Stapling them together makes the picture
  scarier than it actually is.
- They downplay net retention recovery in segments with strong workflow
  ownership. Our own NDR in the dispatch-routing segment is still
  above 110%, and I know of at least three peer companies in adjacent
  verticals where the same is true. The 96% panel median hides huge
  segment variation that matters for how we should respond.
- The TallyBeacon case study is being used to argue something it
  doesn't actually support. The displacement happened because of a PE
  rollup forcing a platform decision — that's a top-of-funnel
  consolidation story, not a seat-expansion failure. I don't think
  this belongs in the seat-expansion chapter at all.

## What I think the meeting actually needs to decide

- **Should we accelerate the move off per-seat pricing for the
  mid-market book?** I think yes, but cautiously. The article makes
  the case more persuasively than I'd been making it internally.
- **Should we change how we frame our exit / portability story?** If
  documented exit really is the new on-ramp to competitors, we need
  to think about whether our current API docs are an unforced own-goal.
- **Do we want to commission our own panel data?** The Vorstmann
  numbers are useful but I want segment-level cuts and they will not
  share them. Probably a six-figure piece of work to do internally.
