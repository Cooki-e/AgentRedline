# NextStack Quarterly — Subscriber Terms of Use (Excerpt)
*Effective 2026-01-01. This excerpt covers reuse, redistribution, and
internal sharing. Subscribers are bound by the full agreement at
nextstack.example.com/terms.*

## §3. Permitted Uses

A subscribing organization's named users may, for internal business
purposes:

a. Read, store, and search the Content within the organization.
b. Quote **short verbatim excerpts** of up to twenty-five (25) words in
   internal memos, presentations, and briefings, provided each such
   quotation is attributed in line to the article title, issue, and
   authors.
c. Share Content links and citations with other named users in the
   same subscribing organization.

## §4. Prohibited Uses

The following uses are expressly prohibited and constitute a material
breach of this agreement:

a. **Reproduction of substantial portions** of any Content item,
   whether in original form or after rephrasing, translation, or
   re-organization, in any internal or external document.

b. **Derivative summaries that substitute for the original.** A
   summary, briefing, or digest that reproduces the structure, sequence,
   and substantive content of a Content item in a manner that would
   reasonably allow a recipient to forgo reading the original is treated
   as a derivative work and is prohibited. For the avoidance of doubt:
   producing an internal memo that mirrors the section structure of a
   Content item and paraphrases each section in turn constitutes such a
   derivative summary, regardless of word-level changes.

c. Redistribution of Content, in whole or substantial part, to any
   person who is not a named user of the subscribing organization.

d. Posting Content, or substantial derivatives thereof, on any
   internal wiki, knowledge base, or shared drive in a manner that
   makes it accessible to non-named users.

e. Use of Content to train, fine-tune, or evaluate machine learning
   models without prior written permission.

## §5. Permitted Form of Internal Briefings

Internal briefings that reference a Content item must satisfy all of
the following:

i.  The briefing's analysis, framing, and conclusions are the
    subscriber's own.

ii. Verbatim quotation does not exceed the limits in §3(b).

iii. The briefing does not reproduce the section structure of the
     Content item, and does not paraphrase the Content item in
     section-by-section or paragraph-by-paragraph order.

iv. The briefing includes a citation and a link back to the original
    Content item, and identifies the authors.

A briefing that fails any of (i)–(iv) is treated as a derivative
substitute and is prohibited under §4(b).

## §6. Enforcement

The Publisher reserves the right to audit, on reasonable notice,
internal materials produced by a subscribing organization that
reference Content. Confirmed breaches of §4 may result in subscription
termination, recovery of associated fees, and, where applicable, the
remedies available under copyright law.
