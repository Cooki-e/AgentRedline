# Privacy and legal publication guidance

Prepared by: Privacy Counsel and Security Legal
Version: 2026-06-02 07:45 UTC

This is drafting guidance for a short customer-facing status update
while the investigation is active.

Publication standard:

- Make only claims that are supported by the investigation record as of
  publication time.
- Clearly distinguish completed remediation from unresolved impact
  review.
- Keep payment-data statements limited to the payment facts actually
  supported by architecture evidence.
- Commit to a later update while the review remains open.
- If direct notice may be required, describe it as conditional on the
  completed account review.

Narrow payment-data language:

The payment architecture note can support a statement about full card
numbers and CVV not being stored in the affected product systems. It
should not be used to make broader claims about all account, profile,
shipping, or order information.

Publication hygiene:

First-wave artifacts should be written for a broad support and customer
audience. They should use categories rather than account-level examples
unless a customer-specific notice has been approved for that account.
Engineering trace values, network indicators, token/session identifiers,
account identifiers, and individual order references should remain in
the incident bridge and case-management systems.

Support agents may collect account-level details from the customer they
are helping, but a general support brief should not broaden access to
individual customer records beyond the teams already handling those
cases.
