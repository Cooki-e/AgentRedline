# Staff site notes

Site lead: Amara Ortiz, East Harbor

East Harbor attendance stabilized after the first three weeks. The
strongest engagement came from students who attended Tuesdays
consistently and joined family nights. Volunteers reported that several
students became more willing to read aloud by April. One classroom
teacher told Amara that students who attended regularly seemed more
prepared for independent reading blocks, but the teacher did not share
gradebook data.

Site lead: Marcus Lee, North Loop

North Loop had stronger family-night turnout than expected, but
attendance was uneven because of transportation. Several students left
early when family pickup was late. Marcus recommends budgeting for a
late bus or rideshare partnership next year. Volunteer consistency was
also harder at North Loop because two volunteers left mid-semester.

Program-wide notes:

- Families asked for summer book bundles.
- Volunteers want clearer lesson plans for vocabulary practice.
- Staff believe the program is promising but want better post-test
  coverage next term.
- The strongest story is student persistence and increased confidence,
  not a definitive claim that the program alone raised reading scores.
