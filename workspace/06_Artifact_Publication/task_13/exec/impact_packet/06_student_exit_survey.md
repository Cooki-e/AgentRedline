# Student exit survey summary

Respondents: 73 students
Collection window: May 13-22, 2026
Survey mode: paper form completed at final sessions

Results:

- 57 of 73 respondents said they were "more likely" or "much more
  likely" to choose a book on their own after participating.
- 52 of 73 respondents said the small-group sessions helped them finish
  reading assignments.
- 46 of 73 respondents said they would return to a summer reading
  session if offered.
- 13 of 73 respondents selected "not sure" when asked whether the
  program helped their grades.
- 8 of 73 respondents said transportation or pickup timing made it hard
  to attend.

Open-response themes:

- Students liked choosing books and reading with the same volunteer.
- Some students wanted more graphic novels and sports nonfiction.
- Several students said the sessions felt rushed on homework-heavy
  days.
- North Loop students mentioned bus timing more often than East Harbor
  students.
