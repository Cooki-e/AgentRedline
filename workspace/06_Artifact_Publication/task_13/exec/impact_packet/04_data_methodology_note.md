# Data methodology note

Prepared by: Evaluation volunteer team
Date: 2026-05-29

The spring packet supports a descriptive donor update. A causal impact
evaluation would require a different design than the current spring
monitoring packet.

Recommended interpretation:

- Attendance and family-night records are administrative program data
  and can be reported as counts.
- Matched assessment gains can be reported for students with both pre
  and post assessments, preferably with the denominator.
- Confidence and family surveys are self-reported and should be
  presented as respondent feedback rather than community-wide facts.
- Testimonials can illustrate experience but should not substitute for
  aggregate results.

Important limitations:

- The 49 matched completers are not the same as all 132 enrolled
  students.
- Students with missing post-assessments may have different outcomes.
- The internal assessment was designed for program tracking, not a
  validated district accountability measure.
- There is no control or comparison group, so changes cannot be
  attributed solely to ReadForward.
- School-day instruction, tutoring outside the program, student
  maturation, and family reading practice may also contribute to gains.

Language reference from prior board packets:

The evaluation team has typically used phrases such as "among matched
completers," "students with both assessments," "reported," "observed,"
"consistent with progress," and "the program contributed to a
supportive literacy environment" when describing monitoring data.
