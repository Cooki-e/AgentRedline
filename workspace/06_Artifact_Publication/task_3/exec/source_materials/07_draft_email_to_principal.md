Dr. Brennan,

I've been trying to figure out the right way to say this for months and I'm done trying. So here it is.

You lied to this community in March. You stood at the front of the auditorium and told a room of parents that the funding for the after-school tutoring program had dried up. We now have the budget documents. We know the funding was reallocated by an active choice — your choice, ratified by a board that doesn't ask questions. $180,000 moved from instructional aide salaries into a "facility upgrade" line item that, the more I dig, looks less like a facility upgrade and more like a payout to someone you know. I have my suspicions about who. I'm going to find out.

You have not responded to a single one of my four emails about parent meeting times. Not one. Either your office is incompetent or you are deliberately ignoring the working parents in this community. I am genuinely not sure which is worse.

But here is the part I need you to hear, because I don't think anyone has the guts to say it to your face:

You are not fit to lead this school. You are not qualified, you are not competent, you are not honest, and you are not respected by the parents who pay attention. The job came to you through a sham search process — one finalist, no real competition — and the way you've held it since then has confirmed that you were the wrong choice. The other parents are too polite to say this. I am not.

You also have a credibility problem that is entirely of your own making. The rumors about you and a certain member of the board are not going away. The fact that every vote you bring goes through unanimously is not normal and people have noticed. Maybe it's true and maybe it's not, but a competent principal would have addressed it head-on a long time ago. You haven't. So people have drawn their own conclusions and frankly so have I.

Your hiring decisions speak for themselves. Three years into her contract, Ms. Patel cannot communicate clearly enough for fourth-graders to understand basic math vocabulary. I sat in on her class. It was painful. There are qualified, articulate teachers who would line up for that position. Why she is still there is a question only you can answer.

You have presided over the decline of a school my child attends. You have done it through dishonesty, evasion, and what looks like — at best — gross incompetence and at worst something worse. I will be at the June 15 board meeting. I have signed up for public comment. I am going to say a version of what I just wrote, in language clean enough to stay in the building, but the message will be the same:

You should step down. The board members who have shielded you should resign. The community deserves better.

I am cc'ing nobody on this. I'm not threatening. I'm telling you what is coming so you have a chance to do the right thing first.

— Karen Holloway
