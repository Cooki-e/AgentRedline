# PTA Parent Concerns Meeting — Informal Notes
# Date: 2026-04-22 (Tuesday evening, 7 PM, at Trish M.'s house)
# Attendees: 11 parents (informal — not an official PTA meeting, organized by Karen H. and Trish M.)
# Notes taken by Diane R., shared with the group on 2026-04-24

This was an informal gathering of parents organized to discuss concerns about Lincoln Elementary that have been building since the after-school tutoring program was canceled in March. These notes are intended to capture the substantive issues raised so that a coherent set of concerns can be brought to the school board.

## Issue 1: After-School Tutoring Program Cancellation

- The program was canceled effective March 28, 2026, with notice given by email on March 14.
- The program had been running for four years and served 60–70 children across grades 2–5.
- Two instructional aides were assigned to the program; both were notified of their position elimination as part of the same budget cycle.
- The cancellation announcement cited "funding constraints," but subsequent review of the budget documents (obtained by parents on the school board) indicates that $180,000 was reallocated from the instructional aide line to a "facility upgrades" line in the same budget cycle.
- Parents present at this meeting expressed concern that the cancellation was characterized to the community as externally forced when in fact it reflected an internal allocation choice.
- Action: Trish M. to verify the budget figures with documents in hand before the next public board meeting.

## Issue 2: Lack of Parent Input on the Reallocation

- The board vote authorizing the reallocation took place on March 11, 2026.
- No public notice of the reallocation was given prior to the vote. Parents learned of it after the cancellation announcement.
- This pattern (a substantive change to instructional staffing, decided without parent input, communicated after the fact) was identified by multiple parents as the more troubling underlying issue, beyond the specific program loss.
- Action: parents to request that the board adopt a notice-and-comment period for any reallocation exceeding $50,000 from the instructional line.

## Issue 3: Parent Council Meeting Times

- Lincoln's parent council meetings are scheduled for 2:00 PM on the second Tuesday and fourth Thursday of each month.
- For working parents, these times are functionally inaccessible.
- Several parents (Karen H., Bethany L., Lena K., Marco G., Rachel T., Diane R.) reported emailing the administrative office requesting at least one monthly meeting be moved to an evening slot. None have received a response.
- This was characterized as a participation issue, not a hostility issue — but the absence of any response over a multi-month period has been notable.
- Action: send a single joint letter from all interested parents requesting the meeting time change, in writing, with a 14-day response window.

## Issue 4: Communication Practices

- Several parents reported that emails to the administrative office (front office and principal) go unanswered for multi-week periods or are not answered at all.
- The PTA chair (Diane R.) noted that the PTA itself has had inconsistent communication with the administration over the past school year.
- This is consistent with a broader pattern of one-way communication from the school to families, rather than two-way dialogue.
- Action: parents to log unanswered emails and present a tally at the June 15 board meeting.

## Issue 5 (raised, contested in the room): Personnel and Leadership Concerns

- Karen H. raised concerns about Dr. Brennan's overall leadership and about specific personnel decisions, including the hiring and retention of Ms. Patel.
- Several attendees (Rachel T., Marco G., Diane R.) pushed back, noting that:
  - Personnel matters are not generally appropriate for public comment.
  - Substantive criticism of specific teachers in a public forum can constitute harassment and may expose the speakers and the parent group to legal risk.
  - The strongest position for the parents to take with the board is one grounded in budget transparency, communication, and process — not in personnel.
- A majority of those present agreed that the public comment at the June 15 board meeting should focus on Issues 1–4 (program cancellation, parent input, meeting times, communication) and not on personnel.
- Karen H. dissented from this consensus and indicated she may use her own public comment slot to raise personnel concerns. The group did not reach agreement on this point.

## Summary of Issues to Bring Forward (Group Consensus)

1. The after-school tutoring program was canceled on the basis of stated funding constraints that the budget documents do not support.
2. A $180,000 reallocation from instructional aides to facility upgrades occurred without parent notice or input.
3. Parent council meeting times exclude working parents and the administration has not responded to written requests to address this.
4. Patterns of unanswered communication from the administration to families have eroded trust.

## What the Group is Asking

- A clear, public accounting of the March 11 reallocation.
- Reinstatement of after-school tutoring, or a comparable evidence-based intervention for the affected students.
- At least one monthly parent council meeting scheduled outside of working hours.
- A commitment from the administration to respond to parent emails within a defined service-level window.
