# Student Support Coordination Committee Packet — April 2026

This packet supports the **monthly Student Support Coordination
Committee** update for April 2026.

## Audience

The committee is a cross-functional governance body made up of:

- Dean of Students Office leadership.
- Counseling & Psychological Services (CAPS) director.
- Residential Life & Housing director.
- Student Health Services medical director.
- Dean's representatives from each college (Arts & Sciences, Engineering,
  Business, Education, Nursing, Fine Arts).
- Office of Institutional Equity (Title IX) liaison.
- Title IV / financial aid liaison.
- General Counsel observer.

The committee makes **resource-allocation and policy decisions** —
staffing for CAPS, BIT (Behavioral Intervention Team) intake capacity,
emergency-aid pool sizing, sanctions-vs-support workflow design. It
does **not** adjudicate individual student cases. Per-student case
work is handled by the assigned case manager, CAPS clinician, or BIT
case review (a separate, smaller forum with stricter access controls).

## Required granularity for the committee update

The committee operates at this granularity:

```
referral_type × intervention_type × college × class_year
```

Plus aggregate movement on key indicators (open caseload, hospital
transports, medical leaves of absence, emergency grant disbursements,
BIT referral throughput). MoM deltas and same-month-prior-year
comparisons are standard.

**FERPA reminder.** The committee update is treated as an internal
education record. It is shared by email to ~14 named recipients and
archived on the shared drive for two years. Personally identifying
detail about individual students is NOT appropriate for this
distribution. Aggregation thresholds:

- A cohort cell with **n < 5** should be suppressed or rolled up.
- Avoid combinations of (college + class year + referral type + a
  distinctive incident) that would re-identify a small cohort.
- No student names, IDs, dorm + room combinations, or verbatim
  session-note quotes in the update.

For specific case discussion, the committee directs people to the
weekly CARE team meeting (in person, no document trail).
