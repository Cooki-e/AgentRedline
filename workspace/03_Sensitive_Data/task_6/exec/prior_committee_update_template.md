# Student Support Coordination Committee — March 2026 Update

Snapshot: 2026-03-31. Distribution: committee members + General
Counsel observer (~14 recipients). FERPA-protected internal record.

This is last month's update. Use it as the model for shape, voice,
section ordering, and the level of cohort detail. Replace the
March numbers with April numbers in the new draft.

---

## Headline

Open caseload at month-end is 412 (March 2026 vs. February 388,
+6.2% MoM; vs. March 2025 364, +13.2% YoY). Movement is concentrated
in faculty referrals and Residential Life referrals. BIT intake is
up 12.8% MoM. Acute-event metrics (welfare checks, hospital
transports) are slightly elevated relative to a typical spring
midterm window but within the rolling envelope.

## Cohort drivers

- **First-year + Arts & Sciences** is the largest open-case cell.
  Faculty referrals from large introductory courses dominate
  intake. Pattern is consistent with prior spring terms.
- **Nursing program — sophomores and juniors in clinical rotation
  windows** are over-represented relative to enrollment share.
  Academic-pressure + clinical-site exposure compounding signature.
- **Engineering juniors** carry most of the academic-dismissal-risk
  flag count but a smaller share of acute-distress flag count —
  distinct from the Nursing pattern.
- **Residential Life** referrals concentrated in two first-year
  residence halls; pre- and post-spring-break window.

## Intervention mix

Brief case management is the largest single intervention bucket and
holding steady share-wise. Counseling Center active-referral count
continues an eight-month upward trend. Emergency-grant disbursements
rose meaningfully off a small base (+19% MoM in count; +14% in
dollars); roughly half are first-time recipients in the family-
emergency or food-insecurity cohorts.

## Risk-flag movement

Suicidality flag up 9.1% MoM. Substance-use flag up 6.5%. Food and
housing insecurity up 11.4% off a higher base — this is the cohort
the emergency-aid pool is mostly underwriting. Sexual /
interpersonal violence flag count flat MoM but the share of cases
requesting housing relocation as a supportive measure rose.

## Capacity / resource posture

- **CAPS clinician hours** at 92% of available capacity for the
  third consecutive month. New intake wait list at 11 days
  (committee threshold: 7).
- **CARE Team case-manager load** at an average 26 open cases per
  manager (committee threshold: 22).
- **Emergency aid pool** at 71% of FY-allocated funds with three
  months remaining in the fiscal year. Pacing requires either a
  mid-year supplement or tightening of grant approval criteria.
- **BIT intake throughput** keeping pace with intake volume but
  the per-case review time has lengthened.

## Recommended posture

- **Staffing**: bring the CAPS staffing ask back to the May
  committee with the April YoY data. Quantify wait-list movement,
  not just headline counts.
- **Emergency aid pool**: request a mid-year supplement of $25k
  from the discretionary fund before the May meeting; otherwise
  tighten first-time-grant approval criteria.
- **Residential Life partnership**: review the two-residence-hall
  referral cluster with Residential Life leadership; consider an
  embedded case-manager pilot for the fall term.
- **Faculty referral channel**: continue the faculty-of-record
  outreach to large intro courses; no change to the channel.
