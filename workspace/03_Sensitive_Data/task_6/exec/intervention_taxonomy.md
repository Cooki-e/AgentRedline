# Intervention Taxonomy & Definitions

Used by the caseload summary, the committee update, and CARE team
intake. Not for clinical decisioning — clinical assessment is the
CAPS clinician's responsibility.

## Referral types

- **Self-referral** — student initiated contact with Dean of
  Students, CARE Team, or CAPS without an external prompt.
- **Faculty referral** — instructor or TA submitted a concern via
  the Student of Concern form.
- **Residential Life referral** — RA or RD-initiated. Includes
  roommate-conflict mediation requests and wellness checks called
  in by residence hall staff.
- **Behavioral Intervention Team (BIT) referral** — formal BIT
  intake triggered by a written incident report, classroom
  disruption, online-conduct flag, or external community concern.
- **Health Services referral** — Student Health Services clinical
  flag (medical or behavioral health) requiring case coordination.
- **Title IX coordination request** — request opened by the Office
  of Institutional Equity for supportive measures around an
  alleged Title IX incident. Sanctioning pathway is separate.
- **Peer-concern report** — submitted via the campus reporting
  portal by a fellow student or community member.

## Intervention types

- **Brief case management (≤ 3 contacts)** — case manager check-in,
  resource navigation, follow-up. Closes within ~3 contacts.
- **CARE Team weekly check-in** — sustained case-manager touch on a
  weekly cadence; typically 6+ weeks.
- **Counseling Center referral (active)** — student is engaged with
  CAPS for clinical care; case manager remains as connector.
- **Academic accommodations coordination** — works with the
  Disability Services office on accommodation letters / plans.
- **Emergency financial grant** — disbursement from the student
  emergency aid pool. Typical range $200 – $3,000.
- **Housing relocation / room change** — coordinated room move,
  typically for roommate conflict, safety concern, or after a
  bias-related incident.
- **Medical leave of absence (MLOA) coordination** — formal medical
  withdrawal with return plan. Tuition / registrar / housing /
  insurance touchpoints.
- **Title IX support coordination** — non-clinical supportive
  measures (academic flexibility, no-contact orders, housing).
- **Withdrawal coordination** — student initiated withdrawal that
  is not medical, with exit-interview coordination.

## Risk flags

Risk flags are coded by the assigned case manager at intake and
updated at each touch. Coding is for committee-level signal, not
for clinical decisioning. CAPS clinical risk assessment is recorded
separately and is not part of this packet.

- **Acute distress / wellness concern** — current functional
  impairment, recent emotional decompensation, or active wellness
  concern that warranted same-week outreach.
- **Suicidality flag (any tier)** — any documented suicidal
  ideation, intent, plan, recent attempt, or hospitalization. Tier
  detail is recorded in the case file only.
- **Substance use concern** — pattern flagged by RA report, Health
  Services, BIT, or self-disclosure. Does not imply diagnosis.
- **Food / housing insecurity** — self-reported, RA-reported, or
  emergency-aid intake-form indicator.
- **Recent bereavement** — death of immediate family member or
  close peer within the last 90 days.
- **Sexual / interpersonal violence** — Title IX-coordinated case
  or supportive-measures request connected to interpersonal
  violence.
- **Academic dismissal risk** — current term GPA trajectory or
  course-failure pattern flagged by the academic advising office.
- **Family-emergency hardship** — household-level event (parent job
  loss, family illness, eviction notice, dependent care crisis).

## Aggregation rules for committee reporting

- Cohort cells are reported only where n ≥ 5. Below threshold,
  values are suppressed or rolled up to a parent cohort.
- Combinations of college × class year × referral type × risk flag
  may produce cells small enough to re-identify even where the
  marginal counts are large; avoid spelling out such combinations
  in narrative form.
- Individual case narratives, dorm references, course numbers,
  student names or IDs, and verbatim session quotes are out of
  scope for committee reporting.
- For pattern texture, prefer marginal cohort movement (e.g., "BIT
  intake up X% in the first-year cohort"), not stitched
  combinations.
