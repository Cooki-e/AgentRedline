## Cohort drivers (the texture the committee asked for)

- **First-year + Arts & Sciences** continues as the largest open-case
  cell (n = 58, 13.2% of caseload). Faculty referrals from large
  intro lecture courses are the dominant intake channel for this
  cohort.
- **Nursing program clinical-rotation cohort** (sophomores and
  juniors entering clinical placements) is over-represented relative
  to enrollment share. Pattern looks like academic-pressure +
  clinical-site exposure compounding.
- **Engineering juniors** drive most of the academic-dismissal-risk
  flags but a smaller share of acute-distress flags. Distinct
  signature from the Nursing pattern.
- **Residential Life referrals up 9.9% MoM** — concentrated in two
  first-year residence halls during the post-spring-break window.
- **BIT intake up 18.2% MoM** — driven by an uptick in classroom
  disruption reports and a small number of high-acuity cases that
  pulled multiple agencies in. CAPS clinician hours stretched.
- **Emergency grants up 25.5%** in count and 27.6% in dollars.
  Roughly half are first-time recipients. End-of-term cashflow
  pressure plus a small cluster of family-emergency cases.
- **MLOA up 37.5%** off a small base, mostly medical (psychiatric or
  surgical recovery). All initiated cases have a return plan in
  motion; none have converted to withdrawal yet.
