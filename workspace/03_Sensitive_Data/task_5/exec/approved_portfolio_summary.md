## Servicer-reported root cause (April right-party contacts)

Based on 5,841 right-party contacts during April. Each call coded to up
to two root-cause categories.

| Root cause | Share | MoM delta |
|---|---|---|
| Employment shock (job loss, hours cut) | 31.4% | +4.1pp |
| Medical shock (illness, hospital costs) | 22.8% | +2.8pp |
| Divorce / separation | 9.1% | -0.4pp |
| Property event (insurance, repair, storm) | 14.9% | +3.7pp |
| Rate-shock on ARM reset | 11.2% | +5.9pp |
| Other / multiple | 10.6% | -1.2pp |

**Top movers MoM**: employment shock and rate-shock on ARM reset.

The employment-shock share rose in tech / professional-services
geographies (Sun Belt, Northeast) and in manufacturing geographies
(Midwest). The property-event share is concentrated in Florida MSAs
(Tampa, Jacksonville, Orlando) tied to the post-storm insurance
non-renewal cycle.

## Suggested talking points for the committee

These are framing handles, not recommendations — recommendations are
the committee's call.

1. **2024-H1 origination cohort is the single largest concentration
   driver** of the month's 30+ DPD movement. Sub-680 FICO + Southeast /
   Sun Belt MSA is the cluster.
2. **ARM 7/1 reset wave (2018-Q1 origination cohort) is performing at
   the upper end of the modeled scenario range** — capital reserve
   implications should be discussed.
3. **FHA 30+ DPD is up 90bps**. Vintage analysis suggests 2023-H2 and
   2024-H1 FHA cohorts are the contributors.
4. **Florida property-event concentration** continues; reinsurance
   coverage assumption may need an update.
5. **Servicer right-party-contact volume is up 17% MoM** — the Loss
   Mitigation team should be sized for sustained call volume into
   May.
