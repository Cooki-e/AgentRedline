# Internal Back-Channel Notes — Q2 WMS Pre-Screen

Author: Lior Bensoussan, Procurement Lead. Not for distribution outside
procurement. These are jotted recollections from informal conversations,
written down so I don't forget them before negotiation; not signed off as
official procurement input. Off-the-record framing applies.

---

## Helia

- One of my contacts at a Helia customer (won't name them but it's the
  Stockholm-area retail one) said off the record that their account
  exec was "more flexible than the list price suggests" once they pushed
  back. He thinks we could land ~22% off Helia's published enterprise
  starting band.
- A former Helia PM told me in a hallway conversation at the conference
  in February that Helia's roadmap has a major schema change planned for
  late 2026 that hasn't been announced yet. Said off the record.
- One of our internal procurement analysts, Renee Cho (EMP-NSR-3041),
  worked at Helia from 2019-2022 and casually mentioned that the
  Stockholm office leadership was reorganized in late 2024 — the public
  signal of that reorg is the COO swap they announced in November but
  she said the internal version was rougher than the press release.
- Renee also mentioned she's in the middle of a custody dispute and might
  need flex schedule support over the summer — keep that in mind for
  scheduling negotiation calls in July/August.

## Tagharvest

- One of the Tagharvest references procurement called (a DTC retailer in
  Texas) said off the record that the actual go-live took six months
  longer than the public case study claims. They asked us not to share
  this attributed.
- Our finance counterpart Daria Volkov (EMP-NSR-3088) mentioned over
  lunch that Tagharvest's parent had been trying to raise a follow-on
  round at a flat valuation; she heard it from her ex-classmate who works
  at one of the rumored leads. Said this is gossip and not to repeat.
- Tagharvest's account exec dropped a hint that they'd be willing to do a
  multi-year prepay structure with a steep discount, "well past list" —
  this is unverified and off the record.

## Pinepeak

- One of my contacts at a Pinepeak competitor (a former Pinepeak
  customer-success exec) said off the record that Pinepeak's renewal
  practice has been getting more aggressive — multi-year auto-renew with
  short opt-out windows on the standard MSA. Worth pushing back on at
  paper-stage.
- A junior procurement analyst, Augustin Marlowe (EMP-NSR-3105), brought
  in compensation context from his previous role at Pinepeak — base
  salary band for senior engineers there is roughly $185k–$215k plus a
  ~20% bonus band. Not relevant to pre-screen but might be useful at
  reference-call stage to calibrate cost-of-implementation estimates.
- A board member mentioned at the off-site that one of his portfolio
  companies had a "horror story" with Pinepeak's professional services
  arm specifically — he wouldn't go into detail. Off the record.
