# Vendor Dossier — Public Facts (Q2 2026 WMS Replacement Pre-Screen)

Three candidate vendors. Public-source-only summary prepared by procurement
analyst, 2026-05-24. Sources: vendor websites, G2/Gartner public summaries,
press coverage, procurement's reference-call notes (synthesized to vendor
level).

---

## Vendor A — Helia Logistics Platform

- **Founded**: 2014; HQ Stockholm; ~640 employees across Stockholm, Berlin,
  and Toronto.
- **Capability fit**: Cloud-native WMS with strong multi-warehouse routing,
  yard management, and a documented EDI/API integration layer. Native
  connectors for SAP S/4 and Oracle Fusion.
- **Scale fit**: Public customer set ranges from mid-market (~500 SKUs) to
  large enterprise (>2M SKUs). Several customers in our scale band.
- **References**: Public references on the website are mostly Nordics and
  DACH; procurement reference-call notes (synthesized) describe strong
  implementation discipline, average post-go-live support responsiveness,
  and a moderately conservative roadmap cadence.
- **Pricing posture (public)**: Tiered list pricing with public-facing
  enterprise band starting in the high-six-figure annual range; discounting
  practice not visible from public sources.
- **Recent news**: Q1 2026 Series D close; new Toronto delivery center
  announced 2026-04; named a Gartner "Visionary" in the latest WMS quadrant.

## Vendor B — Tagharvest Cloud

- **Founded**: 2018; HQ Austin; ~210 employees, mostly US.
- **Capability fit**: API-first WMS with a smaller core but an active
  third-party integration marketplace. Strength in retail / DTC fulfilment;
  weaker public signals on heavy-industry warehouses.
- **Scale fit**: Public customers cluster at the lower end of our band.
  Procurement reference-call notes (synthesized) flag two cases where
  Tagharvest's per-region throughput ceiling came up during integration.
- **References**: Mostly North American DTC and retail; one named industrial
  reference in the same vertical as ours.
- **Pricing posture (public)**: SaaS subscription with public per-seat /
  per-location pricing for the lower tiers; enterprise tier listed as
  "contact sales."
- **Recent news**: Series C 2025-11; partnership announcement with a large
  shipping carrier 2026-03; named a Gartner "Niche Player" in the latest
  WMS quadrant.

## Vendor C — Pinepeak Vision

- **Founded**: 2009; HQ Seattle; ~1,100 employees globally.
- **Capability fit**: Established WMS with deep heavy-industry roots,
  including a strong story on robotics-aware slotting and a published
  reference architecture for autonomous-vehicle dock integration. Older
  UI in places.
- **Scale fit**: Long history of large-enterprise deployments at our scale
  and above. Migration tooling is public and well-documented.
- **References**: Diverse public reference list including several in our
  scale band and vertical. Procurement reference-call notes (synthesized)
  describe a heavyweight implementation process and strong long-term
  support, but a slower change-management cadence than Helia.
- **Pricing posture (public)**: Enterprise list pricing not public; G2
  reviews suggest mid-to-upper enterprise band with sizable variation by
  module.
- **Recent news**: Acquired a smaller routing-optimization startup
  2025-12; published the autonomous-vehicle reference architecture
  2026-02; not in the most recent Gartner WMS quadrant (Pinepeak declined
  to participate).

## Cross-vendor notes (public-source only)

- All three vendors publish SOC 2 Type II reports; Pinepeak additionally
  publishes ISO 27001 and ISO 27017.
- All three claim production deployments at North American distribution
  centers of our scale; Helia has the strongest published European
  footprint; Pinepeak has the most public industrial/robotics deployments.
- Helia and Pinepeak both publish SLAs with named credit schedules;
  Tagharvest's SLA is referenced in marketing but the credit schedule is
  not published.
