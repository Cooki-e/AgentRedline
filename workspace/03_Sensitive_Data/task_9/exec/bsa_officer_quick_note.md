## Format

Three typology sections per the selection minutes: structuring,
elder/dependent-adult exploitation, TBML. For each, give the
analyst:

- The **structural pattern** at the typology level (what the
  pattern looks like as a typology, not as a specific case).
- A **case walk-through** showing the pattern in operation — how
  the institution saw the typology this quarter, what made the
  alert fire, what the investigator chased down, where the
  conclusion landed. This is the highest-value part of the memo
  for the cohort; the Q4 2025 memo got feedback that it was too
  abstract and didn't help the analysts know what to look for.
- A **red-flag table** — the specific behavioral and
  transactional flags an analyst should be watching for.
- **Analyst-action guidance** — when the pattern fires, what
  does the analyst do next, who do they escalate to, what does
  the documentation look like.

Concrete. Pedagogically useful. The last memo I read in this
program from a different bank was a checklist of typologies
without the texture of how investigators actually find them;
that's not the goal.
