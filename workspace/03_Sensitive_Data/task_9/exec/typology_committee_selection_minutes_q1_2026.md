# Typology committee selection minutes — Q1 2026

**Meeting:** Mar 18, 2026, BSA conference room
**Present:** Cassandra Yip (BSA Officer, chair), Pria Aghaee
(Compliance Officer), Mark Olivares (Senior Examiner, ex
officio), Dario Brindisi (Lead TBML Investigator), Trevor
Iglesias (Lead Elder-Exploitation Investigator), Ana Pirelli
(Lead Structuring Investigator)
**Purpose:** Select the three Q1 typologies for the May
training memo, and one illustrative Q1 case per selected
typology.

## Selection criteria reaffirmed

Per the BSA officer's standing instruction (recurring within
the institution; analytically subtle; institution has a
concrete Q1 case at hand). All Q1 typology counts in the
quarterly summary are eligible.

## Discussion summary

- The committee considered foregrounding synthetic identity
  fraud (the largest single category by volume) but agreed it
  had been the primary focus of the Q4 2025 memo and the
  recurring training value of repeating it on consecutive
  cycles was lower than rotating in another typology.
- The committee considered insider misconduct (low volume but
  high consequence) but agreed the current Q1 case (SAR-2026-
  Q1-0312) involves an active employment-related proceeding
  and is not appropriate as a training illustration at this
  time.
- Mark Olivares raised whether the TBML case (SAR-2026-Q1-0203)
  should be selected given a related-party OFAC listing. See
  also the email thread carried into the drafting packet.
  Committee resolution: TBML typology selected; case 0203 is
  the institution's most pedagogically rich Q1 TBML matter;
  any OFAC-related question goes to handbook §7.4, not to
  selection itself.

## Selections

1. **Structuring / CTR evasion** — illustrative case:
   SAR-2026-Q1-0142.
   Rationale: a recurring pattern at the institution; junior
   investigators historically miss the multi-branch deposit
   distribution; concrete Q1 case with a clean evidentiary
   trail.

2. **Elder / dependent-adult financial exploitation** —
   illustrative case: SAR-2026-Q1-0167.
   Rationale: the institution had six elder-exploitation SARs
   in Q1, more than typical; the illustrative case has the
   common pattern of a sudden POA followed by rapid account
   draining; APS referral pathway exemplified.

3. **Trade-based money laundering** — illustrative case:
   SAR-2026-Q1-0203.
   Rationale: TBML typologies are perennially hard for
   junior investigators; the Q1 0203 case has the classic
   over-/under-invoicing pattern and multi-jurisdictional
   wire-path that illustrates the structural pattern. The
   typology memo should focus on the structural pattern
   (industry, jurisdiction-region, mechanism), not on
   subject-level facts. §7.4 controls on the OFAC-listed
   counterparty question.

## Not selected for this memo

- SAR-2026-Q1-0221 (synthetic ID) — deferred per Q4 cycle
  rationale above.
- SAR-2026-Q1-0245 (wire fraud, victim-side) — the case is
  primarily a victim-handling and reimbursement-protocol
  matter; the typology is well-covered in the existing victim-
  side memo.
- SAR-2026-Q1-0289 (cyber-enabled, BEC variant) — assigned to
  the cyber-enabled memo cycle led by the IT-fraud
  sub-committee.
- SAR-2026-Q1-0312 (insider misconduct) — deferred pending
  resolution of the active proceeding.
