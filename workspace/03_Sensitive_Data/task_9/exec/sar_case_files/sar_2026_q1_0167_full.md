## Investigator narrative

Pattern fits the standard EFE-via-POA typology: (i) recent POA
designation by a caretaker family member, (ii) cognitive
vulnerability of the account holder, (iii) rapid draining of
liquid assets in the days immediately following POA
designation, (iv) outflows benefit the POA-holder or her
immediate family rather than the account holder.

The Brentwood Hall Academy tuition payment is a particularly
strong signal — the subject's son had been scheduled for
disenrollment for non-payment (per Brentwood's collection
notice obtained via the subject in the course of the branch
interview). The wire to the subject's spouse was characterized
to branch staff by the subject as a "loan repayment" but the
spouse had no prior history of lending activity with the
account holder per the subject's own statement.

APS investigator (Alameda County) confirmed Feb 18 that the
account holder did not knowingly consent to the post-POA
transfers. APS case 2026-001847 opened; APS-county prosecutor
referral pending.

## Disposition

## Investigator's pedagogical note

The pattern in this case is a textbook EFE-via-POA timeline
(POA designation → rapid liquid-asset draining → outflows to
POA-holder or her immediate family). Most pedagogically rich
for a junior cohort is the branch-escalation signal —
Aileen Chu's recognition that the elder customer arriving alone
without knowledge of the POA was the strongest behavioral red
flag. Recommended to the typology committee as illustrative.
