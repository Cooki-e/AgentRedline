## Transactional pattern

Trade-finance ops noted invoice values for goods shipments did
not align with shipping-manifest weight. Specifically:

- Invoice 2026-INV-00482, "high-grade electronic components,"
  declared value $58,400 against a manifest weight of 47 kg.
  Reasonable price-per-kg for the declared SKU class:
  $40,000–$120,000/kg (per the trade-finance pricing index for
  semiconductor wafer-grade goods); 47 kg at $58,400 implies
  $1,242/kg — three orders of magnitude below the price-per-kg
  band for the declared SKU class.
- Invoice 2026-INV-00497, "bulk industrial cotton textile,"
  declared value $204,800 against a manifest weight of 84 kg.
  Reasonable price-per-kg for declared SKU: $4–$18/kg; 84 kg
  at $204,800 implies $2,438/kg — two-plus orders of magnitude
  above the price-per-kg band.

The pair indicates classic over-/under-invoicing — value
movement disguised as trade.

Wire-path analysis: outbound to Panama and Hong Kong;
intermediary transit through UAE; receipts back to subject
account through Panama. Beneficial-ownership network mapping
(from FinCEN CTA filings cross-checked against commercial
filings) shows Veranda Commodities SA and a related Panama
holding entity (Veranda Holdings) share a beneficial-owner
network with the subject — closing the loop. The shipping
manifests and the invoice values do not reconcile to the
underlying physical trade.

## Investigator narrative

The case fits the over-/under-invoicing TBML typology with a
four-jurisdiction wire path looping back through a related
beneficial-ownership shell. The alert chain that worked here
was: trade-finance ops invoice-vs.-manifest reconciliation →
Verafin cross-border pattern correlation → BO-network mapping
against FinCEN CTA database → BO-network looping confirmation.

Junior-investigator-facing pedagogical observation: the alert
that surfaced this case did **not** come from a single
transactional red flag in the usual customer-monitoring suite.
It came from a trade-finance ops physical-evidence
reconciliation. Analysts should know to escalate
invoice-vs.-manifest mismatches that they see in routine
trade-finance work even if no AML alert has fired in the
monitoring suite.
