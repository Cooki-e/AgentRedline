## High-level pattern (for index purposes only)

- Inbound wire ($87,300) from a commercial customer to a newly
  opened business checking account at this institution.
- Receiving account opened 11 days prior to the inbound wire;
  account opened in the name of an LLC that was registered
  with the California Secretary of State 6 weeks prior to
  account opening.
- Beneficial owner's CTA filing matches the LLC's registered
  agent but does not match any business activity verifiable
  via standard commercial databases.
- Inbound wire was followed within 4 hours by an attempted
  outbound wire to a Singapore counterparty for $84,000
  (gross of the inbound minus a per-transaction holdback).
- Outbound wire halted at the wire-room manual-review step
  after the counterparty's bank notification reached our wire
  ops team. Funds held; receiving account frozen.
- Counterparty bank confirmed the inbound wire was the product
  of an email-spoofing attack on the counterparty's accounts
  payable team; the spoofed email purported to be from a known
  vendor with updated remit-to instructions.

The pattern is a textbook receive-side BEC variant. Detailed
identifiers, the LLC's specific name, the beneficial owner
name, and the spoofing-vector forensic detail are held for the
Q2 cyber-enabled cohort packet.
