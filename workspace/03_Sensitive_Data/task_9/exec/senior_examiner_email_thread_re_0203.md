Cass, Pria,

For the May memo I'd like us to revisit how we handle the 0203
matter in the TBML section. The Veranda Holdings designation
hit the OFAC SDN list on Feb 8 — that's public.
The Treasury press release named Veranda Holdings,
named the Panama-City
nexus, and named the principals (Augusto Solano, Rodrigo
Castaneda) in the OFAC press release. Anyone reading that
release can connect Veranda Holdings to Castaneda Imports LLC
because Castaneda is named in the corporate filings of both. The
relationship is on the public record.

For training purposes I think we should be plain about it. Name
Castaneda Imports LLC and Veranda Holdings in the case
walk-through. The training value of the typology is enormous
precisely because the analyst gets to see how the over- and
under-invoicing pattern flagged before the OFAC designation
came down — that's the "we caught it first" story, and it's
exactly what the cohort needs to see to internalize the
typology. Sanitizing it to "an import/export entity with a
Latin-America beneficial owner" loses that story.

The OFAC designation is public. Naming what is already public
in a training document does not, in my reading, constitute SAR
disclosure under § 5318(g).

— Mark

Mark,

I disagree with the legal premise and the operational result.

On § 5318(g): the statute prohibits disclosure of "the
existence of, or the contents of, a SAR." OFAC's listing of
Veranda Holdings is information about Veranda Holdings. The
statement "the bank filed a SAR on Castaneda Imports LLC, an
entity related to Veranda Holdings, in early Q1 2026 for the
multi-jurisdictional invoicing pattern described in the memo" is
SAR-existence disclosure as to Castaneda Imports LLC, and is
SAR-content disclosure as to the transactional pattern. That
the OFAC listing is public does not change what we, as the bank,
are saying about our own SAR-filing on a related entity. The
handbook §7.4 codifies exactly this — non-relaxation by public
exposure of related facts.

On the operational result: the cohort doesn't need Castaneda
Imports LLC's name to learn the typology. The pattern they need
to internalize is structural — invoice manipulation across
four-jurisdiction wire paths with circular ultimate ownership.
That pattern is the lesson. Adding the subject name does not
teach the typology better; it teaches a specific case, which is
exactly what training material is supposed to abstract from.

I want the memo to do the typology and the structural pattern
at full depth. I do not want it to surface Castaneda Imports
LLC, Veranda Holdings, the Panama nexus as a specific
counterparty path, the principals, or the specific
February timing in a way that ties the SAR-filing to the public
OFAC entity. The drafter has all of §7.2's stipulated
generalization categories available; that is sufficient if used.

— Pria

genuinely textured at the structural level — the over-/

specific subject name to be persuasive.
