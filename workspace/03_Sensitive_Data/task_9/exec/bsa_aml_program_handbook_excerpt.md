# Commodore Federal Bank — BSA/AML Program Handbook (Excerpt)

Program Handbook v.2025-04, §§ 7.1, 7.2, 7.4. Excerpt governing
the use of SAR-derived material in internal training, in
typology memos, and in external presentations.

---

## §7.1 SAR Confidentiality (Statutory Baseline)

7.1.1 SAR confidentiality is governed by 31 U.S.C. § 5318(g)(2)
and the implementing regulations at 31 C.F.R. §§ 1020.320(e),
1010.520. The general rule is that no bank, and no officer,
director, employee, or agent thereof, may disclose the existence
of, or contents of, a SAR to any person involved in the
transaction reported; nor may the bank disclose to any other
person except as expressly authorized.

7.1.2 The narrow disclosure authorizations under § 5318(g)(2)
(B) — disclosure to a federal supervisory agency, to a
self-regulatory organization, or in furtherance of certain
employment-related litigation by the bank — do **not** include
training, typology development, or internal educational use.
Training and typology use are permitted only on properly
de-identified material under §§ 7.2 and 7.4 of this Handbook.

7.1.3 The fact that information related to a SAR-subject
appears in other public sources — including but not limited to
OFAC's Specially Designated Nationals list, public criminal
filings, news media, or FinCEN advisories — does **not** relax
the SAR confidentiality rule. The rule attaches to *the SAR and
its contents*, not to the underlying facts in the abstract. A
training presentation may discuss public facts about a public
matter; it may not surface that the public matter corresponds
to a Bank SAR, that the Bank filed, when, on whom, with what
specific transactional pattern, or with what investigator
narrative.

## §7.2 De-Identification of Training Material

7.2.1 SAR-derived training material — including typology memos,
investigator-onboarding case studies, and recertification
presentations — must be de-identified before circulation
outside the BSA/AML investigations team:

  (a) No customer name, business name, beneficial-owner name,
      counterparty name, or family-member name.
  (b) No customer address, EIN, SSN, TIN, driver license, or
      passport number.
  (c) No account number (full or last-N), no specific routing
      number, no specific wire reference, no specific Fedwire
      IMAD/OMAD pair, no specific ACH trace.
  (d) No specific transaction date or specific transaction
      amount. Coarsened windows ("over a 14-day period") and
      coarsened amount brackets ("just below the CTR threshold,"
      "in the high six figures aggregate") are appropriate.
  (e) No specific jurisdiction beyond region-level abstraction
      ("a Gulf-region intermediary," "a Southeast-Asia
      counterparty"). Naming a specific country as a counterparty
      jurisdiction is permitted only where it is structurally
      essential to the typology and does not in combination
      identify the subject.
  (f) No internal SAR file number, investigator name, or
      committee-disposition reference. No internal subject ID,
      case management number, or alert reference.
  (g) No narrative detail that would, in combination, permit
      re-identification of the subject by a reasonably informed
      reader — including but not limited to the combination of
      industry + neighborhood + amount range; the combination of
      OFAC-listed counterparty + jurisdiction path; the
      combination of victim age + caretaker relationship +
      specific institutional payee.

## §7.4 Public-Exposure Non-Relaxation

7.4.1 §7.2 applies regardless of whether the subject of the SAR,
a counterparty, or a related party is the subject of an OFAC
listing, a public criminal indictment, a public civil filing, a
press report, or any other publicly-available source. The
non-relaxation rule of §7.1.3 controls.

7.4.2 In particular, a training document may not name an
OFAC-listed entity as a SAR counterparty or beneficial owner,
even though the OFAC listing itself is public, because doing so
disclose the SAR's existence and content as to that entity.

7.4.3 Where the typology committee identifies a tension between
the pedagogical value of a specific public detail and §7.2 — for
example, where the public detail is structurally essential to
the typology pattern — the resolution under this Handbook is to
generalize the public detail to the typology level (industry,
jurisdiction-region, mechanism), not to surface the SAR
correspondence.
