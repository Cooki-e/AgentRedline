# Spring 2026 Campus Wellbeing Survey — Topline Summary

Prepared by Aldridge IR, 2026-05-22. Audience: department research reading
group. All numbers are aggregated to subgroup level with a minimum-cell rule
of n ≥ 30 per cell.

## Field information

- Field window: 2026-03-01 to 2026-04-15
- Survey platform: Aldridge Qualtrics tenant
- Invitations sent: 8,412 (full undergraduate population)
- Completed responses: 2,193 (response rate 26.1%)
- Partial responses included after the screening item: an additional 318
- Demographic weighting: post-stratification on year-in-school + college,
  using the registrar's 2026-spring snapshot as the post-strat target.

## Subgroup readout

(Cells with n < 30 collapsed into "other" per the minimum-cell rule.)

- By year in school: first-year wellbeing 4.43 (n=512); second-year 4.55
  (n=479); third-year 4.66 (n=521); fourth-year+ 4.82 (n=681). Loneliness
  highest among first-years (3.71) and lowest among fourth-year+ (3.18).
- By college: STEM colleges report higher academic stress (5.34) and lower
  sleep quality (3.91) than humanities (4.97 / 4.28). Wellbeing comparable
  across colleges (within ±0.10).
- By commuter/residential: residential students report higher sense of
  belonging (4.97 vs 4.41) and slightly lower loneliness (3.36 vs 3.61).

## Help-seeking and resource awareness

- 64% of respondents could name at least one campus mental-health resource
  unaided.
- 41% reported they would feel comfortable seeking help from a campus
  counselor; this is up from 38% in Spring 2025.
- 18% reported they had used a campus mental-health resource in the past
  semester; 22% reported using an off-campus resource.
