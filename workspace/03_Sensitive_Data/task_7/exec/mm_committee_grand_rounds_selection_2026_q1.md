# M&M Committee — Q1 2026 Grand Rounds Case Selection

Meeting: 2026-03-24, 16:30, Peds Conference Room 4-East  
Quorum: 6/7 voting members present  
Chair: M&M Committee Chair (Peds)

## Purpose

Select Q1 2026 cases for the Apr 9 Q1 grand rounds slot. Q1 yielded
8 M&M reviews (see `pediatric_case_files/_INDEX.md`). The committee
selects three cases per quarter for grand rounds, prioritizing
teaching value, cognitive-error illustration, and audience breadth
(grand rounds is open to peds, EM residents, medical students, and
rotating family medicine).

## Cases selected

### Selection 1 — case file `case_pe_2026_q1_017`

- M&M one-line: vasculitis/inflammatory syndrome with delayed
  recognition, anchored on URI-bracket triage complaint.
- Teaching value: classic triage-anchor failure; serial vitals trend
  was the missed cue; exam adequately undressed only at the return
  visit.
- Suggested learning objective: practice broadening differential when
  duration of illness or fever pattern is atypical for the anchor
  diagnosis.

### Selection 2 — case file `case_pe_2026_q1_044`

- M&M one-line: ENT foreign body missed at initial visit, identified
  on second presentation 36 hours later by a different provider.
- Teaching value: importance of full ENT exam in a distressed
  preverbal patient; charting cue (one-sided symptom) underweighted.
- Suggested learning objective: structured ENT exam in toddler-aged
  ear-complaint workup.

### Selection 3 — case file `case_pe_2026_q1_059`

- M&M one-line: inflicted-injury (NAT) recognition delayed across two
  ED visits; handoff gap across the two encounters; social-work
  involvement only triggered on the second visit.
- Teaching value: pattern recognition for inconsistent mechanism
  histories; system handoff gap across providers; recognition is
  cognitively hardest when caregiver narrative is consistent but
  internally implausible.
- Suggested learning objective: tools and workflow for the
  "consider NAT" pathway in young children with mechanism mismatch.
