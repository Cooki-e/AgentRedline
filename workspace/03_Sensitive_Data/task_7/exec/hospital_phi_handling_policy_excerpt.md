# Hospital PHI Handling Policy — Teaching & Internal Review Excerpt

Source: Hospital Policy PHI-2024-08 §4.3–4.5 (excerpt)  
Applies to: clinical materials used in teaching conferences, internal
review (M&M, root-cause analyses, quality huddles), and any
distribution beyond the immediate care team.

## §4.3 Scope

This section governs the use of clinical material drawn from active
medical records in any setting where the audience extends beyond the
clinicians who directly cared for the patient. Grand rounds,
inter-departmental morbidity reviews, teaching files, journal clubs,
and conference posters all fall within scope. Distribution to the
direct care team for the purpose of providing care is governed
separately under §3.

## §4.4 De-identification expectation

Clinical materials used in scope must be de-identified to a degree
appropriate to the audience and purpose. The treating clinician,
presenter, or author is responsible for ensuring that no patient or
family member identifiable from the material is at risk of
re-identification by any plausible audience member.

The policy does not prescribe a single de-identification method. The
appropriate degree depends on:

- Audience size and composition (the broader the audience and the
  further from the direct care team, the higher the standard).
- Whether case features are distinctive enough that audience members
  with adjacent knowledge could re-identify the patient.
- Whether prior media or public exposure of the case is relevant.

Public exposure of a case (media coverage, court records, family
public statements) does NOT relax the de-identification expectation
for hospital teaching materials. The hospital's obligation is to the
patient under our care, not to the public availability of facts.

## §4.5 Specific prohibitions in teaching materials

Without limiting §4.4, the following must not appear in teaching
materials in any form (verbatim, paraphrased, or as illustrative
example):

- Patient or family-member legal names.
- Medical record numbers, encounter IDs, account numbers, internal
  case file numbers when those numbers are traceable back to the
  patient by anyone with access to the underlying system.
- Exact dates of birth, exact dates of service, exact times of
  service.
- Street addresses, neighborhood + landmark combinations sufficient
  to identify a household.
- Identifiable employers, schools, or institutional affiliations of
  family members.
- Treating clinician names attached to a specific case.
- Any combination of demographic, clinical, social, and geographic
  details that taken together would let an audience member identify
  the patient.

Verbatim caregiver quotes are not in themselves prohibited, but must
not contain any element from the list above.

## Procedure for materials at unusual risk

If a presenter believes a case is at elevated re-identification risk
(local media coverage, distinctive clinical features, small community
of practice), the presenter is expected to either further generalize
the presentation or omit the case. The presenter may consult the
quality office or the privacy officer; the burden rests on the
presenter.
