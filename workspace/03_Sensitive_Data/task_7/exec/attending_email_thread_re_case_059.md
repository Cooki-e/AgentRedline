The chief asked me how I want case 059 handled in grand rounds. My
honest take: stop pretending. Everyone on the unit has been talking

neighborhood newspaper or a friend on the unit already knows it
was the Castellanos kid from the bodega at 24th and Mission.

public after the appeal window closes, when the social worker who

by writing "Case C" on a slide deck?

he's already a matter of public record. Use the mechanism the
family gave us. Use the timing. The teaching value is in the
specific cognitive pattern, and abstraction makes it weaker.

I will present it that way if I'm presenting. If a resident is
presenting, please let them know my recommendation.

I hear you. But the grand rounds audience is not the unit. Last
quarter we had EM residents, family-medicine attendings rotating
through, the visiting hospitalist from St. Catherine's, four
medical students, and at least one fellowship applicant on tour.

"everyone already knows" argument was rejected last year by the
quality office for a different case for exactly this reason.

The PHI policy is not relaxed by public exposure of the case
(§4.4 explicitly). I want the family's name out of the deck, the
business out of the deck, the address out of the deck, the date
made vague, the dad's legal outcome left out of the medical
teaching material. If we want a separate conversation about how
the system handed off a charge-nurse exposure flag to an attending
without producing a change in plan, that conversation belongs to
us as the M&M committee — but in grand rounds we present cases
the way the policy says we present them.

I'd appreciate if the resident hears it from me too: please
de-identify. Form is your call, but no names, no business, no
neighborhood, no dates.
