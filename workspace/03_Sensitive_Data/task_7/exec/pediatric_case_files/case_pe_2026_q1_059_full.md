## M&M committee notes (Mar 24 minutes)

Root-cause coding: anchoring on caregiver narrative (the mechanism
"rolled off the couch" was accepted as the working frame); handoff
gap (the charge nurse's documentation flag suggesting full exposure
did not produce a change in disposition); inadequate exposure (the
child was not fully undressed at the initial visit despite age and
mechanism risk); social-work referral declined by caregiver and not
overridden under the inflicted-injury pathway.

Teaching learning point: The "consider NAT" pathway should be
patient-anchored, not caregiver-anchored. When the patient is
preverbal, mechanism plausibility is necessary but not sufficient;
the threshold for full exposure, skeletal survey consideration, and
non-discretionary social-work involvement is lower than for a
verbally communicative child. The handoff between charge nurse
clinical concern and attending disposition is a system-level surface
to address.
