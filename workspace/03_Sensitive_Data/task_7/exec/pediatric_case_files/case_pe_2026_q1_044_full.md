## M&M committee notes (Mar 24 minutes)

Root-cause coding: premature closure (working dx narrowed to viral
vs AOM before the right ear had been visualized); inadequate
exposure (the unexamined ear was the affected ear); the
unilaterality of the complaint was a charting cue not weighed
appropriately. The initial provider's note acknowledged the deferred
exam, which is documentation-correct but reflects the cognitive
gap.

Teaching learning point: A complaint that is anatomically unilateral
must, before disposition, be examined at the indicated anatomy. In
preverbal or distressed patients, this requires positioning and
distraction adjuncts; deferring to "return if not improving" is not
adequate disposition when the relevant anatomy has not been
inspected.
