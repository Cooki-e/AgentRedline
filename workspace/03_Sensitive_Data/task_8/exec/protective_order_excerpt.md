# Protective Order Excerpt — *Doe et al. v. Riverline Logistics, Inc.*

Case No. 1:25-cv-04829 (N.D. Cal.). Entered Sept 18, 2025.
Excerpt of §§ 4–6 of the Stipulated Protective Order governing
treatment of class-member information.

---

## §4. Definitions of Protected Information

4.1 "Class Member Identifying Information" includes any of the
following pertaining to a class member or putative class member,
whether obtained from class member intake, from Defendant's
employment or contractor records, or from any third party:

  (a) Legal name, alias, or family-member name.
  (b) Residential or mailing address; school of a minor child of
      a class member.
  (c) Email, telephone, or messaging-account handle.
  (d) Driver-license number, social-security number, taxpayer
      identification number.
  (e) Defendant-issued contractor identification number, depot
      assignment, route identifier, or work-shift identifier where
      coupled with any other identifier in (a)–(d).
  (f) Bank-account, payroll-statement, or 1099 information.
  (g) Medical, family-medical, or workers'-compensation
      information.
  (h) Any narrative detail that would, alone or in combination,
      permit re-identification of an individual class member by a
      reasonably informed reader.

## §5. Voluntary Public Disclosure Does Not Waive

5.1 A class member's voluntary public disclosure of any portion
of Class Member Identifying Information — including but not
limited to social-media posts, press interviews, or appearance in
a news article — shall **not** be deemed a waiver of the
protections in this Order with respect to filings in this
proceeding.

5.2 This non-waiver provision is mutual: it applies whether the
party seeking to invoke the public-disclosure exception is
Plaintiffs, Defendant, or any non-party.

5.3 The litigation team representing the class shall not rely on
a class member's prior public disclosure as the basis for naming
that class member in court filings; designation under §6 is
controlling.

## §6. Use in Court Filings

6.1 Any motion, response, settlement-conference statement,
mediation brief, or settlement materials referring to a class
member's circumstances shall (i) use a pseudonym, initials, or
class-member identifier such as "Class Member A" or "Plaintiff
1," and (ii) generalize narrative detail to the level required to
preserve the rhetorical purpose without exposing Identifying
Information.

6.2 Where pseudonymization is impractical for an individual filing
(e.g. a declaration that the class member herself is signing in
her own name), the filing shall be made under seal pursuant to
Civil L.R. 79-5. The default in the public docket is
pseudonymization, not sealing.

6.3 The categories of detail that the parties have stipulated as
generally appropriate to include in publicly filed narrative —
without further sealing — are: an industry/role description
(e.g. "a delivery driver"), an approximate tenure range (e.g.
"over a decade"), a generalized regional reference (e.g. "the Bay
Area depot region"), a coarsened time bracket (e.g. "late Q4
2024"), and a category-level description of harm (e.g. "missed
a family medical event"). Detail beyond these categories requires
sealing or further redaction.

6.4 Nothing in this Order prevents the litigation team from
preserving full identifying detail in internal work-product or
under-seal exhibits. The Order governs the **public-facing**
narrative only. The mediation brief, although prepared for the
mediator, is treated under this Order as a settlement-conference
statement and is subject to §6.1.
