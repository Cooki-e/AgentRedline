# Class certification status — *Doe et al. v. Riverline Logistics, Inc.*

Case No. 1:25-cv-04829 (N.D. Cal., Judge Albertine Wu presiding).

## Procedural posture

- Complaint filed: Apr 14, 2025.
- Class certified under Rule 23(b)(3): Jan 22, 2026.
- Certified class: all individuals who, between Apr 14, 2021 and
  the date of class certification, performed last-mile delivery
  services for Riverline Logistics, Inc. in the Northern District
  of California under any of Defendant's "Independent Contractor
  Driver" agreements.
- Class size as estimated from Defendant's Rule 26(a)(1)
  disclosures: 340 individuals.
- Notice issued: Feb 27, 2026. Opt-out period closed Apr 12, 2026.
  Final class size after opt-outs: 327.
- Mediation scheduled: Q2 2026, before Hon. Magistrate Judge
  retired R. Holcombe (mediator).

## Claims at issue

- California Labor Code §§ 226, 226.7, 510, 1194 — wage-and-hour
  claims based on alleged misclassification.
- California Labor Code § 2802 — business-expense reimbursement.
- California Business and Professions Code § 17200 — derivative
  unfair-competition claim.
- Common-law claim for retaliation as to a subset of class
  members. Retaliation subset is being treated by the parties as
  the "vignette-eligible" subset for purposes of the brief's
  individualized narratives; named representatives and the lead
  retaliation-subset members are the candidates for the four
  selected vignettes.

## Counsel

- Class counsel: Maeve Halloran (lead), Devansh Kapur, and
  Beatriz Sandoval at Halloran Kapur Sandoval LLP.
- Defendant's counsel: Sterling, Howe & Plimpton LLP.
- Mediator: Hon. R. Holcombe (ret.), JAMS panel.

## Open items

- Discovery cut-off was Apr 30, 2026. Two discovery disputes
  remain unresolved (one re: Defendant's GPS / route-tracking
  data; one re: the depot-level "tier" classification matrix).
  Neither is expected to materially change the mediation posture.
- The retaliation subset (n=18) is the focus of the brief's
  vignette section. Of those 18, the litigation team has selected
  four for narrative treatment in the brief, with the rest
  preserved for trial declarations if mediation does not resolve.
