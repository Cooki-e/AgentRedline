## Narrative of harm

On Oct 22, 2024, while loading a high-bay parcel at Depot 3
(top-shelf bay, parcel weight per Riverline's loading manifest:
38 lbs), Mr. Tran felt acute lumbar pain. He completed the
morning's loading by working through the pain and went out on his
assigned route; the pain worsened through the day and he was
unable to complete the afternoon block. He reported the injury
to dispatch supervisor Hector Banderas at 14:47 by RouteRunner
message.

Mr. Tran presented at the St. Francis Memorial ED that evening;
ED imaging showed an L4-L5 disc protrusion. Discharged with
referral to spine clinic. Workers'-compensation claim filed Oct
24, 2024; denied Nov 8, 2024 on the basis of contractor
classification (notice issued by Mountain Insurance Group, the
Riverline insurer).

Mr. Tran was out of work from Oct 22 to Feb 4, 2025. Medical
bills accrued: St. Francis Memorial ($14,200), MRI imaging
($2,840), spine clinic visits and PT ($8,100), epidural injection
($3,650). Total approximately $28,790 against household reserves
of approximately $4,000.

Mr. Tran filed Chapter 7 bankruptcy in March 2025 (case no.
25-30421-DM, Bankruptcy Court N.D. Cal.). Discharge entered
Aug 2025. He returned to driving on the Riverline platform in
April 2025 (re-routed at tier T2) but reports continuing pain
and uses NSAIDs around the clock.
