## Narrative of harm

Twelve-plus years on the Depot 2 early-shift block. Routes
covered the Oakland-East-Bay early-shift commercial drop block;
Ms. Okonkwo was known at the depot as a reliable senior driver.
In Q4 2024, Riverline rolled out the "tier-rebalancing initiative"
across the Northern California depots — depot operations managers
were given discretion under the contractor agreement to "rebalance
routes" without notice. Ms. Okonkwo's regular block was rebalanced
in three waves over Oct–Nov 2024; her weekly stop count fell from
~520 to ~210. Weekly take-home dropped from ~$1,520 to ~$580.

Two consecutive months below her monthly rent of $2,180 at the San
Pablo Ave address. Three-day notice posted Dec 11, 2024;
unlawful-detainer filing in Alameda County Superior Court
docketed Jan 7, 2025 (case no. 25CV-LDR-00417). Ms. Okonkwo and
her family vacated Jan 28, 2025; doubled up with her sister
Adaeze Okonkwo in Berkeley through early March; signed the
current 1842 Adeline lease in April 2025.

Her teen child changed schools mid-year (from Oakland Tech to
Berkeley High) as a downstream consequence; her younger child
moved from Hoover Elementary to Rosa Parks Elementary.

Ms. Okonkwo was not nominally retaliated against — Riverline
maintained she was simply "rebalanced." But the rebalancing
followed a Sept 2024 conversation in which she had asked the
depot manager about the overtime-exemption status of her hours,
on behalf of two other drivers who had asked her to inquire.
