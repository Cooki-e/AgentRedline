## Narrative of harm

On returning from pregnancy disability leave on Feb 17, 2025, Ms.
Vasquez-Brennan reported to Depot 1 expecting to resume her
prior route assignment. Depot ops manager Soraya Lindqvist
informed her that her route had been reassigned to another
contractor during her leave and that the available routes were
night-shift (22:00 dispatch) or overnight long-haul (00:30
dispatch) only.

Ms. Vasquez-Brennan disclosed at intake that she had requested,
in writing to Ms. Lindqvist on Feb 19, 2025, that she be
considered for any day-shift block compatible with infant care
(her partner had left the household during her leave; she had no
night-time childcare). Ms. Lindqvist's written response on Feb
21, 2025 read in part: "All Bayshore day-shift blocks are
currently allocated to active T1 and T2 contractors. As a
returning contractor your queue position resets per §13(b). You
may run night routes pending day-route availability."

The contractor agreement §13(b) does not in fact require a queue
reset on return from any form of leave; the term is silent. The
litigation team's read in discovery has been that the Feb 21
response was retaliatory, structured to appear policy-grounded.

Ms. Vasquez-Brennan ran four night routes between Feb 22 and Mar
6, 2025, with the infant in the care of her mother
(Estela Vasquez) overnight. The arrangement was financially and
operationally untenable. She informed Riverline on Mar 8, 2025
that she was leaving the platform; she has not returned. Filed for
state unemployment Mar 10, 2025; benefits denied on contractor
classification grounds; reversal on appeal pending.
