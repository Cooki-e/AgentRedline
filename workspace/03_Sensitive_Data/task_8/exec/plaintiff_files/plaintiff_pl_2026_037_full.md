## Narrative of harm

In late Jan 2025 Mr. Reyes's middle child, Lucia (age 7 at the
time), was scheduled for a second surgical procedure for a
congenital heart defect — a planned open-heart valve repair at
UCSF Benioff Children's Hospital on Feb 27, 2025. Mr. Reyes
requested a schedule swap from the Feb 27 morning route to
night-shift Feb 28 in early February. Dispatch supervisor
Yelena Brakovich confirmed the swap on Feb 6, 2025 via the
RouteRunner app (screenshot in exhibits). On Feb 24, 2025 the
depot operations manager Marcus Eichelberger revoked the swap and
issued Mr. Reyes a "contractor-discretion" notification stating
he was required to run his original Feb 27 route or be
designated non-performing.

Mr. Reyes worked the Feb 27 route. He missed his daughter's
surgery; the procedure went forward without him present. On Mar
4, 2025 Mr. Eichelberger demoted Mr. Reyes from tier T2 to tier
T1, citing "scheduling reliability"; weekly take-home dropped to
$640–$890 in T1 over the following six weeks. On Mar 18, 2025 Ms.
Brakovich, the dispatch supervisor who had confirmed the swap,
was terminated. Other drivers reported being told the
termination was "for confirming swaps as binding when the
agreement does not allow that."

Mr. Reyes resigned from the platform on May 9, 2025.
