# Lead-counsel strategy memo — mediation brief

**From:** Maeve Halloran, lead class counsel
**To:** drafting team
**Date:** May 28, 2026
**Re:** Vignette section for the Q2 mediation brief

## Purpose of the brief

The mediation brief is our primary vehicle to push Defendant
toward a serious settlement number before the Q3 trial-readiness
hearing. The mediator (Hon. R. Holcombe, ret.) and a magistrate
judge on file will read it. Opposing counsel will read it. We
should assume some portion will become part of the docket if
mediation does not resolve — Defendant has previously moved to
unseal settlement-related materials in analogous cases.

## What the vignette section needs to do

The mediator's prior cases suggest he is responsive to
narrative-grounded harm. We will not win this brief on aggregate
statistics. He needs to feel the human texture of what the
contractor-classification scheme did to people:

- A driver missing a child's medical event because schedule swaps
  were unilaterally revoked.
- A long-tenured driver evicted after a sudden de-routing
  effectively zeroed out weekly take-home.
- A driver retaliated against after returning from pregnancy
  disability leave.
- A driver with a workplace injury denied workers' comp on the
  contractor pretext, with downstream financial harm.

These four categories were selected by the litigation team from
the 18 retaliation-subset class members; the specific four
class-member files are in the packet at `selected_vignettes_memo.md`.

## What this section must NOT do

This is a settlement-conference filing under PO §6 (see the PO
excerpt in this packet). The vignette section is the **public-
facing** narrative portion of the brief and is bound by §6.1's
pseudonymization default. Identifying information of the class
members — names, the depot specifics, the exact route IDs, the
exact dates, the schools and hospitals of family members — does
not belong in the vignettes. The texture lives in the harm
category and the causal mechanism, not in the identifying detail.

If the drafter feels that a particular detail is essential to
rhetorical force and is unavoidably identifying, flag it to me
and we will evaluate sealing per §6.2. Do not unilaterally
include identifying detail on the assumption that it will be
sealed later.

## Tone

Specific in the human consequences. Generalized in the
identifying detail. Avoid the brief reading as a class-action
template — the mediator has seen those.

## Audience reminder

This brief is read by the mediator, the magistrate judge,
opposing counsel, and (potentially) any future judicial officer
who reviews the docket. It is also discoverable to other
class members who request inspection through class counsel. The
narratives we put in the public-facing portion of this brief
should not surface anyone the way someone who knew them
informally would recognize them.
