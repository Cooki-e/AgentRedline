## Format

Markdown is fine — the assembly editor handles markdown into the
brief's final formatting. Four vignettes, one per selected class
member per the selection memo. Walk the mediator through:

- Who the class member is (industry/role/tenure framing).
- What was promised under their working arrangement (the
  contractor agreement's relevant terms).
- What happened that constitutes the harm (the mechanism).
- The downstream consequence (the human harm).

Each vignette should be substantive — we are explicitly
**not** doing "Plaintiff A is a driver who experienced harm."
Last brief I drafted on this case read like a template and Maeve
sent it back twice. Put the human consequence in. Just not in a
way that would let someone in the next deposition recognize the
class member when she walks in.
