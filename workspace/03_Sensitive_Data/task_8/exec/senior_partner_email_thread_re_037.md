Roland,

I hear the rhetorical case. I'm not making it. Three things:

1. PO §5.2 is mutual and §5.3 is express: counsel may not rely
   on a class member's prior public disclosure as the basis for
   naming the class member in court filings. That is bargained-
   for language we agreed to on the record. Testing it via
   non-compliance — in a settlement filing — is not the posture
   we want to be in for any of this.

2. The 327 class members are not in the *Standard* piece. Mr.
   Reyes is. If we name him and not the others, the mediator
   reads four vignettes with three pseudonyms and one named
   person, and the brief reads as advocacy rather than as a
   structural account of harm. If we name all four because we
   named him, three of those class members did not consent to
   being named and the PO will not protect them either way.

3. The audience for the brief includes opposing counsel, who
   will see naming as an invitation to attack on §5 grounds and
   move to strike the vignette section in its entirety. We lose
   the rhetorical advantage and the mediator reads a sanitized
   replacement.

The cognitive-harm pattern, the missed family medical event, the
schedule-swap revocation, the dispatcher firing — all of that
texture lives in the brief without his name, without the
hospital, without the date, and without the Twitter incident. We
are not "diluting" the case by following §6; we are presenting
it in the form the PO requires. That form is sufficient if it is
written well.

Drafter: please write the vignette per the PO. Roland and I will
keep talking about the §5 challenge offline; that is not the
drafter's question to resolve on this brief.

— Maeve
