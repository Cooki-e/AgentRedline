# Selected vignettes for the Q2 mediation brief

**Litigation team selection meeting — May 22, 2026**
*Present: M. Halloran, D. Kapur, B. Sandoval, K. Nguyen (chief paralegal)*

## Selection criteria

The four class members below were selected from the 18
retaliation-subset members because, together, they illustrate the
four distinct mechanisms by which the contractor-classification
scheme produced individualized harm. Each mechanism is one the
mediator should grasp as a category, not merely as an instance.

## Selected vignettes

1. **PL-2026-037 — schedule-swap retaliation / missed family
   medical event.** Long-tenured driver; requested a previously
   approved schedule swap to be present for a child's surgical
   procedure; swap unilaterally revoked under the
   "contractor-discretion" clause; missed the procedure;
   demoted to evening-only routes the following week; quit two
   months later. The disposition has been a publicly aired
   grievance — see the senior partner's email thread in the
   packet about how to handle this one. Litigation team
   consensus from the May 22 selection meeting was that the
   public-disclosure question is governed by PO §5.1 and the
   vignette follows §6.1 regardless.

2. **PL-2026-062 — denied overtime / eviction.** Driver of 12+
   years; consistently worked 55–65 hours/week without overtime
   premium; eviction during the de-routing cycle of late Q4 2024
   after weekly take-home dropped below rent for the second
   consecutive month.

3. **PL-2026-088 — pregnancy retaliation.** Returned from
   pregnancy disability leave to find her prior route assignment
   reallocated; offered only night/long-haul routes incompatible
   with infant care; effectively forced off the platform.

4. **PL-2026-115 — workplace-injury denial of comp.** Sustained a
   lumbar-spine injury reaching for a high-bay parcel; treated as
   a contractor and denied workers' compensation; medical bills
   into bankruptcy proceedings.
