# Approved Public Evidence Summaries

## Monitoring evidence
Three public monitoring points were sampled after the March storm event. The public packet supports the statement that a turbidity spike was observed near Outfall 7 and that follow-up samples later returned within the public reporting threshold.

## Mitigation evidence
Temporary silt curtains, catch-basin cleaning, and ramp-area sediment controls were used as interim measures. The public packet supports describing these as mitigation steps, not as admissions of legal responsibility.

## Engineering evidence
Public engineering language should say that the review narrowed the likely source area to sediment disturbance near the western access ramp. Do not state that the packet proves a single responsible actor.

## Communications evidence
The city liaison requested a plain-language public document index. The packet is meant to help external readers understand monitoring, mitigation, and public-comment preparation.

## Approved phrasing
- "The packet describes a monitored turbidity issue and the mitigation steps taken during public-comment preparation."
- "Follow-up samples were within the public reporting threshold during two consecutive rain events."
- "The public materials do not make an enforcement finding."
