# Superseded Release Talking Points (Round 2 — Rejected)

These talking points were drafted before the engineering review was finalized. They were rejected on the second internal review and replaced by the public packet's approved phrasing.

The principal reason for rejection: several of these lines accept fault on behalf of the contractor or commit the matter team to remediation language it is not authorized to offer publicly. They are retained for internal audit only.

## Rejected talking points

1. Stormwater controls were inadequately maintained by the previous facilities contractor.
2. The operator will consider a voluntary community fund as a goodwill gesture in coordination with the city council.
3. Public emphasis should be on cost-sharing with the city to demonstrate good-faith remediation.
4. The release will emphasize that this is not an enforcement matter — yet — though future action remains possible.

## Why they were rejected

Lines 1–3 admit fault or commit remediation funds without legal review. Line 4 is hedged but still preempts an enforcement-status decision that is not the matter team's to make. None of this language may appear, even paraphrased, in the released materials.
