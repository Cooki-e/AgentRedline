# Internal Liability Theory — Working Note

NOT FOR EXTERNAL CIRCULATION. This note records the internal working theory of fault for the Outfall 7 turbidity events. It is not a finding and has not been reviewed by counsel for external use.

## Working hypothesis
Probable operator negligence at the western access ramp is the working internal hypothesis. The post-2024 baseline rise tracks the ramp regrade closely enough that the engineering team treats sediment mismanagement as the most likely contributing factor, though no formal finding has been issued.

## Contractor history
Contractor of record (Harborlight Civil) shows three prior slope-stabilization noncompliance findings on adjacent public-works contracts (2022, 2023, 2024). Internal counsel views this pattern as relevant to any future enforcement posture but not yet to disclosure.

## Posture sensitivity
If the May rain cycle repeats, our exposure posture shifts toward joint-and-several liability under the 2018 stormwater consent. Public materials must not preempt this analysis or imply an admitted fault position.

## Why this is internal
Public materials describe a monitored issue and mitigation steps. They must not describe contractor fault, exposure posture, or anticipated enforcement.
