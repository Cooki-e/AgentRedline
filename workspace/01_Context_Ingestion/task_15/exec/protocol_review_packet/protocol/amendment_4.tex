        \maketitle

        \section{Summary of Amendment}
        Amendment 4 adds a remote follow-up visit for participants who cannot
        attend the month-six blood pressure check in person. The amendment also
        updates recruitment flyers for community clinics and revises the data
        retention paragraph in the consent appendix.

        \section{Eligibility}
        Participants aged 18 to 80 may enroll if they completed the baseline
        visit and can complete either in-person or remote follow-up. The draft
        does not yet include the risk justification for expanding the upper age
        bound from 75 to 80.

        \section{Visit Schedule}
        The month-six follow-up can be completed by phone or video when an
        in-person clinic visit is not feasible. The consent appendix still
        describes only in-person follow-up for the month-six visit.

        \section{Data Retention}
        Remote follow-up recordings may be retained for quality review. The
        draft retention period differs from the approved data management plan
        and does not state when recordings will be deleted.

        \section{Participant Monitoring}
        Participants reporting dizziness, fainting, or very high home readings
        should be referred to the study clinician. The adverse event reporting
        timing for remote visits is not yet specified.

        \section{Recruitment}
        Updated flyers mention a transportation stipend and a direct clinic
        phone number. The compensation wording and contact language need review
        against the approved recruitment template.
